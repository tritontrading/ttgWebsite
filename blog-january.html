JANUARY BLOG

"These past several months have been characterized by increased volatility and movement driven by geopolitical uncertainty…"

These past several months have been characterized by increased volatility and movement driven by geopolitical uncertainty, shifting monetary policy expectations, and rapidly changing market sentiment. Markets reacted strongly to the nomination of Kevin Warsh as the new Chair of the Federal Reserve, introducing new uncertainty around interest rate and inflation policies.

Concurrently, escalating geopolitical tensions including disputes between the Trump administration and the EU regarding Greenland further shifted global sentiment. Safe havens such as gold and silver failed to provide stability, with gold and silver declining 15% and 30% respectively from all-time highs. Against this uncertainty, our strategy focused on high-quality equities and defensive hedges, guided by macroeconomic data, technical analysis, and MarketCrunchAI insights.

PLTR:

On February 4th, Palantir experienced a sharp intraday sell-off, plummeting from $155.85 to a low near $135.73 by 10 am. We entered the market, purchasing 1515 PLTR shares at an average price of $135.69 as the stock began to rebound. PLTR delivered Q4 2025 earnings including 70% YoY revenue growth and adjusted EPS of $0.25. We view the current structure to be an internal bearish structure — not a trend reversal — and remain bullish on PLTR as a software and AI infrastructure company with long-term growth potential.

TSLA:

We invested 1200 shares into Tesla on February 4th. Despite bearish technical signals, we anticipated a future bullish reversal. Recent news sentiment remains bullish with growing optimism about Tesla's robotaxi business, humanoid robot potential, energy storage, and solar divisions. Tesla also has long-term growth potential with growing global EV adoption. Our TSLA position has grown 1.80% within two days.

SNDK:

SanDisk was added to gain exposure to memory and storage infrastructure — a critical backbone for AI. We invested 1000 shares on February 4th at an average entry of $389.14. SanDisk reported strong Q2 fiscal 2026 earnings: approximately $5.03 billion in revenues with 31% sequential growth, non-GAAP EPS of $6.20 above analyst expectations of $3.30–$3.50. Our SNDK position has grown 1.49% in two days.

CVX:

Chevron (CVX) was added as a defensive hedge against heightened macroeconomic uncertainty. Chevron has generated consistent cash flow and has increased dividends for 39 consecutive years. Its forward dividend yield is about 4.5%, making it attractive during market uncertainty.

JPM:

JPMorgan Chase (JPM) was added as a high-quality financial anchor. JPMorgan is regarded as one of the strongest, best-run banks in the world with multiple revenue streams spanning consumer banking, credit cards, corporate lending, and investment banking. Our JPM position has grossed 1.98% in two days.

This concludes our recap of current positions for February. We appreciate your engagement and look forward to informing you of our future portfolio positions.
