DECEMBER BLOG

"We had a surge of profits, due to strategic trades made using MarketCrunch.AI"

As the new trading week begins, let's take a look at the trades we made powered by MarketCrunch AI. For the week from December 8th to 12th, 2025, we had a surge in profits due to the strategic trades made using MarketCrunch.AI along with precise ChatGPT prompts that helped us calculate which trades had the most upside and potential.

NVDA:

On December 10, 2025, we entered a Nvidia (NVDA) position by purchasing 5,000 shares at $185.55 via a market buy. Later that same day, we exited with a market sell at $182.86, resulting in a loss of approximately -$13,450. The market shifted amid growing skepticism around long-term AI spending and competitive pressure from DeepSeek and Google's newest models.

Recognizing the continuing downward trend, we strategically shifted to capitalize on downside momentum through options. We executed a limit buy on a put option for NVDA at $3.77 for 100,000 contracts (NVDA260102P00175000, $175 strike, Jan 2, 2026 expiration). Our predictions were accurate — the stock continued declining and we sold the contract at $5.60 on December 12, resulting in a gain of $183,000 overall — a 48.5% return, validating our use of MarketCrunch.AI's analytical predictions.

TSLA:

We are currently holding 3000 shares of Tesla (TSLA), bought at $445.65. Our position has generated a profit of approximately +$32,790.00, representing an estimated 2.99% gain. As of December 12, 2025, Tesla closed at $458.96, confirming the upside momentum we anticipated. We invested based on short-term strength, clear momentum, and alignment with AI-driven market signals.

META:

On December 8, we sold 347 shares of Meta Platforms (META) at $668.97, locking in a profit of $9,105.28. These shares were originally purchased at $642.73. While Meta reached an all-time high of $796.25 in August, the stock had since decreased significantly. Reality Labs posting an operating loss of $4.43 billion in Q3 2025 raised concerns that ultimately led to our decision to sell.
